Auditor-Free Byzantine-Resilient Federated

Introduction

System Architecture

Federated Learning (FL) trains a shared model across many clients without exposing raw data, but remains
vulnerable to Byzantine model-poisoning attacks, where adversarial clients manipulate gradients to disrupt convergence or induce targeted misclassification. This is amplified in Privacy-Preserving FL (PPFL),
where encrypted gradients block direct inspection.
Existing defenses rely on auditor-based architectures that cluster and score updates before aggregation.
Auditors add:
• Significant computational and communication overhead,
• Cryptographic complexity and latency,
• A single-point-of-failure trust assumption.

Contributions
• An auditor-free Byzantine-resilient PPFL framework using lightweight Functional Inner Product (FIP)
geometry on encrypted gradients.
• Two-stage geometric audit – direction then magnitude – with linear O(nd) complexity.
• State-of-the-art targeted accuracy (97.69%) on MNIST non-IID, surpassing the best auditor-based baseline without any external auditor.

Threat Model
• Server: honest-but-curious aggregator.
• Adversarial clients: up to 50% Byzantine; label-flip (targeted) and scaled-magnitude (untargeted) attacks.
• Attacker goal: degrade accuracy or force targeted misclassification.
• Defender goal: detect malicious updates without decrypting individual gradients.

Two-stage geometric audit on Paillier-encrypted gradients: (3) direction filter removes label-flip attacks; (4) magnitude window
catches scaling attacks; (5) only validated ciphertexts are aggregated. Audit scalar and final aggregate are the only decryptions
— individual gradients remain encrypted end-to-end.

Methodology
The framework embeds Byzantine resilience directly into server-side aggregation — no external auditor

Experimental Setup
• Dataset: MNIST, non-IID partitioning across clients
• Model: Logistic regression
• Encryption: Paillier additive homomorphic encryption
• Attacks: Targeted (label-flip) & untargeted (scaled); attack rates 10%, 20%, 30%, 50%
• Metrics: Target Accuracy (Tacc), Other-label Accuracy (Oacc), Overall Accuracy (ACC)

Privacy Guarantees
• Per-client gradients remain encrypted end-to-end; never decrypted at the server.
• Exactly two decryptions per round: per-client audit scalar and the final aggregate.
• Audit scalar reveals only the projection onto the trusted direction — not the gradient.
• Private key held only by the Trusted Key Authority.

MNIST non-IID across attack rates 10%–50%. Defense holds ≥ 97% targeted accuracy through 30% attack rate; convergence
preserved at 50%.

Key Highlights
✓ No external auditor needed
✓ O(nd) complexity vs O(nd 2)
✓ 97.69% targeted accuracy (SOTA)
✓ Gradients stay encrypted end-to-end

This work presents an auditor-free, Byzantine-resilient federated learning framework that leverages
functional inner product geometry and robust statistical thresholding to detect malicious updates in encrypted gradients. Experiments on MNIST show strong robustness against targeted and untargeted poisoning attacks while reducing computational and communication overhead. Future work will validate scalability
on IID settings and broader datasets.
